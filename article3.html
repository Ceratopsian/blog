Industry Professionalism

by Josh Boyd | 20/10/2024

Understanding Professionalism

Professionalism is generally defined as:

“The skill, good judgment, and polite behavior expected from someone trained to do a job well.” – Merriam-Webster

These principles provide a foundation for behavior and ethics in any career, including web development.

Pillars of Professionalism

• Expertise and Continuous Learning - According to Daniel Goleman in Emotional Intelligence, true professionalism involves ongoing skill enhancement and self-improvement. In fields like web development, this pillar translates to a commitment to learning new technologies, following industry standards, and refining technical abilities. This pursuit of expertise helps professionals remain valuable contributors in an ever-evolving field.

• Ethical Integrity - Integrity, discussed by Stephen R. Covey in The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People, involves honesty and moral clarity. Professional integrity means aligning actions with values and maintaining transparency. For web developers, this includes honest assessments of one’s skills, fair client billing, and respect for intellectual property. Integrity ensures a trustworthy, ethical work environment.

• Reliability and Accountability - Drawing from Peter Drucker's The Effective Executive, reliability is essential for professionalism. This pillar emphasizes delivering on promises, meeting deadlines, and taking ownership of one's work. Accountability, in Drucker’s terms, is about recognizing mistakes, making improvements, and working to ensure that outcomes meet or exceed expectations. Web developers with this sense of responsibility contribute to smoother, more trustworthy project outcomes.

• Respect and Collaboration - Respecting others' perspectives and effectively collaborating are key principles in professionalism, as outlined by Susan Cain in Quiet: The Power of Introverts in a World That Can’t Stop Talking. For web developers, this means valuing team input, providing constructive feedback, and fostering a positive environment where diverse skills thrive. Respecting others promotes innovation and project success through collaborative effort.

Professionalism In Web Development

But does professionalism specifically apply to web development? Yes, the field has its own professional guidelines, which help ensure fair practices, quality work, and collaborative success. For example, the Web Professional Standards of Conduct promote ethical practices and set standards for behavior in this industry.

One organization with a relevant code of ethics is the International Association of Web Professionals (IAWP), which, like the International Game Developers Association (IGDA), advocates for ethical conduct. IAWP promotes principles in areas such as quality work, client respect, fair pricing, and transparency in skills and project goals.

The IAWP standards are divided into three main areas:

1] Professional Principles: Web developers are encouraged to promote equal access to technology, uphold high-quality standards, and stay transparent about their skills. Equal opportunity is key, and these values help prevent discrimination while promoting a healthy, inclusive work environment.

2] Workplace Guidelines: Just as IGDA’s workplace guidelines emphasize safety and anti-harassment, IAWP’s guidelines protect developers by setting standards for workplace safety and fair treatment. In web development, this could mean creating a workspace that is free from harassment, respecting work-life balance, and ensuring mental well-being.

3] Leadership Standards: In leadership, web development follows similar rules, such as supporting employees’ well-being, offering fair timelines, and setting achievable goals. Leaders in the web industry have a responsibility to provide a balanced workload and supportive work culture.

Applying Professionalism to Your Work

For web developers, professionalism is essential for career advancement and project success. Adhering to ethical standards, continually improving technical skills, and treating others with respect all contribute to a professional web development career. Following the standards outlined by organizations like the IAWP helps establish credibility and fosters positive industry practices.

Whether working independently or in a team, professionalism is as crucial in web development as in any other industry, ensuring quality work, accountability, and ethical behavior throughout the development process.
